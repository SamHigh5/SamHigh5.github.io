Topic: The Life of a Shrine Maiden

History

Miko's or Shrine Maidens have existed for centuries. They used to revered as powerful shamans. Over the years a Miko's role has changed. Miko's no longer preform the role of shamans. They now preform rutials and ceremonies at their Shrine. While a Miko's duties have changed they are still viewed as an imprtant tradtion. Nowadays many Shrine Maidens are students or young women working part-time at shrines.

Clothing

Miko's wear red to symbolize vatality and white to symbolize purity.

  • Hakui: A white kimono-like robe symbolizing purity.
  • Hibakama: Bright red plaeted trousers or a long, divided skirt.
  • Tabi: White split-toed socks.
  • Zori or Geta: Traditional Japanese sandals.
  • Chihaya: A white coat decorated with colorful designs or patterns worn over the usaul attire for ceremonies and dances.

Training

  • Shrine etiquette and proper conduct.
  • Basic knowledge of Shinto beliefs and practices.
  • Ceremonial procedures and rituals.
  • Performance of Kagura and other sacred dances.
  • Handling of Shrine offerings and amulets.

Duties

Physical Duties

  • Kagura Performaces: a sacred dance preformed to appease Shinto deities.
  • Shrine Maintenance: cleaning and tidying the buildings and grounds.
  • Ritural Offerings: prepareing and presenting offerings, including food, sake, and symbolic objects within designated areas.
  • Exorcisms and Purification: removal of evial spirits or negative energies from people or places.

Interpersonal Duties

  • Divination and Oracle: provide guidance and predictions to shrine vistors.
  • Assisting Priests: working closely with priests and assisting them with preparation and completion of Shrine ceremonies and rituals.
  • Welcoming and Guiding Vistors: offers assistance and information like the Shrine's history, customs, and rituals to vistors.
  • Educatyion and Training: undergo training from senior Miko's to learn rituals, dances, and ceremonies specific to their shrine.

Kagura

Kagura is a sacred Shinto dance. Kagura means "god-entertainment and is believed to orginate from a dance prformed by a Shinto Goddess. Now Kagura dances are preformed at Shrine festivals and ceremonies.

The modern Kagura dance has many purposes:

  • Honoring and pleasing the Kami.
  • Purifying the Shrine and its visitors.
  • Telling stories from Shinto Mythology.
  • Inviting the presence of Kmai during rituals.
